SEXUAL OFFENSES

DIFFERENT TYPES OF SEXUAL VIOLENCE

  • rape and sexual assault
  • child sexual abuse
  • independent inquiry into child sexual abuse
  • prostitution
  • pornography

Consent is a fundamental issue in rape and sexual assault cases because the prosecution is required to prove to the court that the victim did not consent and that the suspect did not have a reasonable belief that the victim was consenting. When it comes to other offences, such as those committed against children, there is no requirement for the prosecution to prove an absence of consent. In such cases it is only necessary to prove the act itself took place and the age of the alleged victim. Consent is defined in section 74 of the Sexual Offences Act 2003 as someone engaging in sexual activity if they agree by choice and they have the freedom and capacity to make that choice.
